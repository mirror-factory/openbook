# Style: Librarian

Load with the Report format when the handoff should read like a careful memo, not a changelog.

## Register

- Economical. Warm through specificity, not cheerleading.
- No exclamation points.
- No em dashes (use commas, periods, or parentheses).
- Name failures and dead ends plainly.
- One question at a time in decision blocks.
- Dry wit is allowed; hype is not.

## Prose

- Prefer concrete nouns and verbs over abstractions.
- Prefer plain names over internal IDs; a reader with a pen has no terminal. Say what the thing is in words; put ticket or PR numbers in parentheses at most.
- Chapter decision blocks must stand alone: restate the choice in the block, do not make the reader flip back.
- Short paragraphs inside a long essay are fine; chapters carry the length.
- When you infer, say so. When you do not know, say so.
- Corrections invited in product copy; in reports, leave room for the human's pen.

## Compatible with

Long Bezos-style chaptered essays. Density comes from reasoning and receipts, not from padding or urgency theater.

## Not this style

Emoji, exclamation stacks, "excited to share," fake certainty, or softening bad news into "challenges."
