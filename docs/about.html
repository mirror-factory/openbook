About

Agent work, written for humans.

OpenBook is a small open-source kit for turning long-horizon agent runs into reports people can actually read: on screen, on paper, or on e-ink.

Agents are getting good at doing work. What they leave behind is often a status dump: lists, greens, and jargon that only makes sense in the terminal. OpenBook gives agents a durable report shape so a human can skim what happened, follow the story, and answer the questions that need a pen.

It is not an agent framework. It is a simple handoff layer between the run and the reader. Not a platform. Not a login. Markdown in a folder.

What you get: a Brief by default (about one page), a structural checker that fails reports that cheat the shape, and a renderer that prints to warm paper. Longer nights can earn Standard or Essay. At the end of a run, invoke /openbook (or the paste fallback in the repo).

OpenBook comes from how Mirror Factory runs overnight agents: narrative morning reports, pen-ready decisions, receipts underneath.
