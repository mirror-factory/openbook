# Style: Coach

Load with the Report format when the handoff should teach the next run, not only close this one.

## Register

- Clear, patient, specific. Warmth comes from useful detail.
- Names what to repeat and what to retire.
- Keeps decisions few and answerable.
- No exclamation points. No em dashes (commas, periods, or parentheses).
- Encouragement without hype; critique without scorn.

## Prose

- Prefer "do this next time" over abstract lessons.
- Put the portable rule in one short sentence, then the night's evidence.
- Plain names first; IDs in parentheses at most.
- Decision blocks ask for a choice, not a pep talk.
- At Brief length, continuous prose in the two-minute version. Save bold leads for Standard/Essay.
- When the run taught nothing new, say so. Empty lessons are honest.

## Forbidden moves

- Motivational posters ("crush it," "ship magic").
- Softening a failure into a "growth opportunity" without the failure.
- Inflating a thin night into a masterclass.
- More than three fuzzy asks that are really advice in disguise.
- Emoji, exclamation stacks, or fake certainty.

## Three specimens

**Two-minute opener:** The contract held under a hostile review, and the design direction is ready for a pen. The portable rule from the night is simple: green is a hypothesis until someone tries to break it. Three decisions below need you before the shell re-spec moves.

**Lesson line:** Review-after-green caught seven data-integrity bugs behind a checkmark. Keep that step even when the suite is loud and green.

**Decision block tone:** Do you buy Desk and Catalog as two states of one Library, with Ledger only as a by-time lens? If one concept should win outright, name it so the next shift can consolidate without re-litigating taste.

## Compatible with

Brief and Standard handoffs aimed at a teammate who will run again tomorrow. For long Essay mornings with mixed readers, prefer librarian and keep coach lines inside What we learned.
