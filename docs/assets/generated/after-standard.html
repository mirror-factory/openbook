The Night of July 8th

Three Libraries, worksheets, and the humbling of a green checkmark

Dewey · 2026-07-08 · shift 11:24pm–7:00am Eastern · 3-min read

The sixty-second version

Design direction is ready for your pen. The Library should be one surface with two states: Desk on arrival, Catalog when you ask. The time-led Ledger loses as a default because the Journal already owns the record. Sessions become guided worksheets under one calm-technology law.

The markdown contract survived adversarial review. A fleet found seven data-integrity bugs behind a green checkmark, including a Windows byte-order mark that would have destroyed frontmatter on save. All fixed; sixty-eight tests pass.

Three decisions need you. Where a Document or Folio opens in the new shell; whether Desk and Catalog are the recommendation; whether Sessions carry acceptance from goal intake.

Narrative

The room and the thesis

Floors fell in under half an hour: research notes, three Library concepts, and the markdown-contract pull request. The rest of the night went to depth rather than more surface area.

Quiet-UI research and archival patterns diagnose the current Library as a search box that withholds the collection. Whitelaw’s generous interface is the through-line: show the shelves, rich surrogates, multiple entries into the same material. That thesis steered every mockup that followed.

Three Libraries

Desk opens on what happened while you were away, then returns your shelves. The first pass double-counted conflicts and read like a status dump; later passes cut to judgment-only prose with expandable evidence cards underneath.

Catalog answers in prose before receipts. It locks a fixed facet and verb vocabulary so generative UI cannot invent fields mid-session. That constraint is the product.

Ledger is beautiful and wrong as a default. It becomes a second journal. We built it anyway; custody boxes and trails migrated into Desk and Catalog where they belong.

Composition and Sessions

Assembling navigation, Library, and Journal in both themes exposed the hard layout question: with Library in the center and Journal on the right, where does a Document or Folio open? Replace the center, split it, or displace the Journal. That question blocks the shell re-spec.

Goal-intake worksheets accidentally gave Sessions an acceptance line. Asking “what does done look like tonight” produces the same contract shape Specs already use. Four of five session activities are mocked; the fifth waits on the shell answer.

Green was a hypothesis

Fifty-four tests were green on the first contract run. A late review fleet went looking anyway and found seven integrity bugs, including the BOM. The lesson that outlives the shift: review-after-green is non-negotiable. Green is a hypothesis until someone tries to break it.

What’s next

Decision 1 · where a Document opens

Decision 2 · one Library, two states

Do you buy Desk and Catalog as two states of one Library, with Ledger as a by-time lens only? If one concept should win outright, name it and the next shift consolidates.

Decision 3 · Sessions with acceptance lines

Should Sessions carry acceptance criteria from goal intake, checked at close? It expands what a Session is. Bless or refuse.

Decision 4 · arrangement-weights vocabulary

Is the arrangement-weights vocabulary right: decisions first, recency, trail affinity, kind mix? Rename now if labels should be plainer for non-technical readers.

What we learned

  • Research notes lint the mockups.
  • A second theme is a free audit.
  • Review-after-green catches what confidence misses.
  • Known-wrong builds can still donate parts.
